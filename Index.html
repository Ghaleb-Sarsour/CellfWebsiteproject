Cell Organelles:

Overview:

Cells make up every living thing on the earth. If thats true, then what makes them up? The many important parts of the cell that allow the cell to function properly are called organelles. While some organelles are only found in specific cells, each organelle has an important role to play in the cell.

The Cytoskeleton:


Image Source

The Cytoskeleton is protien filaments in cytoplasm. Its goal is to give shape and structure to the cell.

The Nucleus:


Image Source

The nucleus is known as the 'brain' of the cell. Inside the nucleus, there is the nucleolus, which contains rDNA. The nucleus stores DNA as chromatin within the nuclear envelope.

The Endoplasmic Recticulum:


Image Source

The Endoplasmic Recticulum(E.R) is split into two parts: the rough E.R and the smooth E.R. The smooth E.R synthesises lipids and the rough E.R synthesises protiens (ribosomes).


Ribosomes:


Image Source

Ribosomes are particles that are found in the cytoplasm or rough E.R with mRNA and RNA. Their job is to link amino acids with peptide bonds.

The Golgi Apparatus:


Image Source

The job of the golgi apparatus is to package protines in vesicles to transport them thoughout the cell. The golgi apparatus also modifies protines.

Vesicles:


Image Source

Vesicles are short lived sacs that transport materials. They are distributed by the Golgi Apparatus.


The Mitochondria:


Image Source

The mitochondria is a cell with a double membrane to produce energy. This energy is called ATP.

The Vacuole:


Image Source

The vacuole has two different forms depending on whether it is in a plant or animal cell. In an animal cell, it is small and stores waste. In a plant cell, it is large and stores water and other molecules.

Lysosomes:


Image Source

Lysosomes contain enzymes to digest wastes, viruses, and bacteria.


Centrioles:


Image source

Centrioles are spindle fibers of protiens that are used for cellular reproduction.

The Cell Wall:


Image Source

Cell walls are only found in plant and fungus cells. The cell wall is a polysaccaride that is used to protect plant and fungus cells.

Chloroplasts:


Image Source

Chloroplasts are organelles only found in plant cells. Their job is to create energy for the cell though the process of photosynthesis.
